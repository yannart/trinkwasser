Sodium regulates the water balance.

How much sodium is suitable for whom, depends greatly on the individual from: athletes is sodium-rich water to recommend people with hypertension or infants should drink low-sodium water (less than 20 milligrams per liter).

A lack of sodium expressed in physical weakness, nausea and muscle cramps way to circulatory collapse.

Recommended are at least 550 mg of sodium per day. For a medically recommended low-sodium diet or for infants less than 20 mg / l of sodium are suitable.

Potassium maintains the water and electrolyte balance in balance, regulates blood pressure and is important for a regular heartbeat and the nerves.

At a potassium deficiency, it may after major fluid losses, z. B. by prolonged diarrhea, vomiting or prolonged intake of drainage and laxatives.

Too much potassium in the blood can lead to heart failure, as potassium inhibits the contraction of the heart muscle.

Calcium is needed for the formation of bones and teeth. Also for the muscles and blood coagulation, calcium is important.

Of calcium is often lacking, which can lead to a decrease in bone strength and thus to osteoporosis.

Magnesium influences the energy metabolism, the interaction of nerves to muscles for protein synthesis as well and bone mineralization. Furthermore, it is responsible for the activation of over 300 enzymes in the metabolism.

Too much magnesium is highly unlikely. In large quantities of mineral causes a softer stool.

Chloride is a component of table salt and is therefore responsible for the regulation of water balance. Also for the nerves, the heart and muscles, as well as acid-base household bleach is important.

Since it occurs in many foods, more than enough chloride is usually added. A permanent oversupply of chloride can cause high blood pressure.

Nitrate is converted to nitrite in the body toxic. In addition, may arise from nitrites carcinogenic nitrosamines.

The nitrite itself may cause an oxygen deficiency of all organs and tissues. Infants are particularly vulnerable for this.

Therefore, the limit value in mineral water is 50 mg / l. The nitrate levels in drinking water in the region are contained from 5 to 38 mg / l below the limit and are therefore classified as harmless.

For comparison: Bodensee-water contains only 4 mg nitrate per liter.

Sulfate promote digestion, even laxative from a level of 1200 mg / l.

Contains a mineral more than 1200 mg / l and at the same high accounts concentrations of minerals such as magnesium, calcium, fluoride, hydrogen and carbon dioxide, it is referred to as healing water.

However, infants and patients with digestive tract diseases and tendency to form uric acid stones should have little sulphate to take (content <240 mg / l).

But even water with a little sulphate has something good: It will taste usually perceived positively. High sulfate contents, however express themselves in a sweet and bitter taste.

Why miss some municipalities on the addition of water from Lake Constance?

It is cheaper for municipalities when they obtain their drinking water exclusively from its own sources.

The municipality Jagsthausen example provides its residents with 100 percent natural water.

Complaints from the community there would be no, many residents have a water softener. Links to the Bodensee water is connected in the case of Jagsthausen with high investment costs and not an issue.

Why not taste my drinking water as well as bottled water?

The taste of water is determined by the minerals it contains.

Water with a lot of sodium and potassium, is perceived as salty taste. Low sodium water - like the Heilbronner drinking water - however, tastes mild.

An unpleasant taste can occur when the pipes are contaminated in the building. Stadtwerke control the quality of drinking water only up to the respective front door. In the house, the responsibility rests with the building owner.

What does the water hardness for me?

Wherever hard water flows slowly or is heated to about 60 degrees, lime settles. In the kitchen and bathroom so arise lime stains, washing clothes more detergent is needed, some household appliances must be decalcified regularly. This is from a water hardness of 21st

In Heilbronn, the upper limit of the water hardness at 18 degrees. Healthwise hard water does not pose any danger, on the contrary, because of the high calcium and magnesium content, it is healthier than soft water.

Why is there no soft drinking water with us?

Most of the Heilbronner Bubbler is in the same rock layer Gipskeuper, who is responsible for the very high magnesium and calcium content in the water. Since both of these minerals determine the hardness of the water, there are in Heilbronn soft water.

The Heilbronner water has a hardness of up to 28 degrees of German hardness, in some of the 40 well water flows at 30 degrees German hardness.

How are the different degrees of hardness?

The taste of water is determined by the minerals it contains.

Drinking water contains varying amounts of minerals calcium and magnesium - depending on whether it is surface water, ground water or spring water.

The more calcium and magnesium in the water are, the harder it is.

Unit ° dH - "degrees of German hardness"?

The unit ° dH (degrees German hardness) is outdated, but in practice common still. Besides ° dH millimoles per liter (mmol / l) was used.

Areas:

  • soft: less than 1.5 millimoles of calcium carbonate per liter (equivalent to less than 8.4 degrees of German hardness)
  • medium: 1.5 to 2.5 millimoles of calcium carbonate per liter (equivalent to 8.4 to 14 degrees of German hardness)
  • hard: more than 2.5 millimoles of calcium carbonate per liter (equivalent to more than 14 degrees of German hardness)

About Careers

As the water-Tool has arisen

Drinking water is considered the best-controlled foods in Germany. And yet we know very little about the water that comes out of the tap at us. Many municipalities publish the test results on its websites. These data are available for the citizens - but not tangible. What does a hardness of 9? 200 milligrams of calcium per liter lot or a little? As mineral kingdom is our tap water, only becomes apparent when we compare it with water from the market.

This is now possible for the region Heilbronn. The idea came in late February at the Open Data Day in Heilbronn. Although many data are available online, they had to be placed in a machine-readable form first. Many municipalities publish only PDF files with the annual averages. These are not machine-readable and had to be carried by the editors. To program the application, a single record is required. On some websites of municipalities which sought information on the drinking water were hidden on sub-pages, outdated or incomplete. In many towns and cities, we have therefore requested the data via email. Only three municipalities came so far no response.

It was surprising to us how different the drinking water is composed in the region. Partially the mineral content within the village differs greatly - namely when there are different service areas. Municipalities as Bad Friedrichshall, Neckarsulm or Zaberfeld are divided into several areas. This was a particular challenge in the phase of data collection. The regions had to be partially allocated to the streets yet, so that the user gets the correct values ​​for its own road appears. The drinking water prices provides the State Statistical Office a complete data set for all counties - for drinking water quality, there is such a public record does not exist.

The application of these zugrundliegende record was therefore a complex research project. Not less complicated were implementation and visualization for the developer Felix Ebert, Ulrich piercing and Tom Görner. A large role was played by user-friendliness and design. For dry figures an interactive graphics should arise, through which the user gets access to his drinking. For the graphical elements Heiko was not responsible.

The drinking water application is a beta version. In particular, the data collection can never be completed. Many municipalities publish soon the current average annual values ​​- they should also be updated in our application. We are dependent on the cooperation of the town halls and have set up a contact area. Due to the large dataset can cause erroneous information. Who discovered Auffälligkeifen can contact us anytime. We look forward to receiving your feedback.

Data and code of the application are open to the public and available under an open source license.
